Knee Rehab — Daily
🦵 Knee Rehab Protocol
Left knee · IT band · flat-foot compensation
▾
Do these daily — 10 min, any time (before bed is great). They target the IT band tightness, glute-med weakness and ankle stiffness behind your left knee pain. Stop any move that sharpens pain.
1. Clamshells with Band
3 × 15 each side · light band above knees
Lie on your side, knees bent 45°, feet together. Rotate the top knee up like a clamshell, squeeze the glute, lower slow. Directly fights the internal knee rotation from your flat feet.
2. Side-Lying Hip Abduction
3 × 12 each side · bodyweight or band
Lie on side, bottom leg slightly bent. Lift the top leg to 45°, toes pointing forward (not up). Lower slowly. Builds the glute med — your key knee stabiliser.
3. Ankle Circles + Alphabet
2 min each ankle · daily
Foot raised, draw slow circles with your toes — 10 each way. Then trace the alphabet with your big toe. Loosens tight ankles so your knee stops compensating.
4. Standing IT Band Stretch
3 × 45 sec each side
Cross your left leg behind your right. Push your left hip out and reach your left arm up and over. Feel the stretch down your outer left hip and thigh. Breathe deep. (Great with the liniment + massager.)
5. Towel Calf + Ankle Stretch
2 × 45 sec each foot
Sit, leg straight, loop a towel around the ball of your foot, gently pull toes toward you. Then point and flex 10×. Frees the calf/ankle tightness driving knee strain.
6. Banded Glute Bridge
3 × 15 · band above knees
Glute bridge with a band above the knees — push your knees OUT against the band as you drive your hips up. Trains the knee NOT to cave inward. Hold 2s at the top.
